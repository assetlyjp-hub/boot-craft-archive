## Overview

Choosing between the Danner Bull Run ($190) and the Tricker's Bourton Country Shoe ($540) is a common dilemma. Both are excellent options, but they cater to different preferences and priorities.

## Danner Bull Run

- **Brand**: danner
- **Leather**: Full Grain
- **Construction**: Goodyear Welt
- **Made In**: USA
- **Price**: $190

The Danner Bull Run is a genuine American-made work boot that delivers heritage quality at an accessible price. Built in Portland, Oregon with full-grain leather and Goodyear welt construction, it features a comfortable wedge Christy sole that excels on hard surfaces. The Ortholite footbed provides all-day comfort for working professionals.

## Tricker's Bourton Country Shoe

- **Brand**: Tricker's
- **Leather**: Calf
- **Construction**: Goodyear Welt
- **Made In**: UK
- **Price**: $540

The Bourton is the shoe version of the legendary Stow boot. Same superb construction and brogue detailing in a derby format. The chunky silhouette and Dainite sole handle any terrain with aplomb.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

### Leather Quality
Both boots use quality leather, but full grain and calf offer different characteristics in terms of patina, durability, and maintenance requirements.

### Construction
Goodyear Welt vs Goodyear Welt — each method has its strengths. Consider your priorities around resolability, water resistance, and break-in comfort.

### Value
At $190 vs $540, the price difference of $350 may be a factor in your decision. Consider the long-term cost-per-wear rather than just the upfront price.

## Verdict

Both are excellent choices. The Danner Bull Run edges ahead for those who prioritize its specific attributes, while the Tricker's Bourton Country Shoe is the better choice for those who value what it uniquely offers. You genuinely cannot go wrong with either.
