## What Is Nubuck?

Nubuck is top-grain leather that has been sanded or buffed on the grain side to create a soft, velvety texture. Unlike suede (which uses the flesh side), nubuck uses the stronger grain side, making it more durable. It offers a luxurious tactile experience but requires careful protection from water and stains.

## Tanning Process

Nubuck is produced using chrome tanning methods by Various. This process gives the leather its distinctive character and determines how it ages over time.

## Care Recommendations

To maintain Nubuck leather:

1. Brush regularly with a horsehair brush after each wear
2. Condition every 4-8 weeks depending on wear frequency
3. Use shoe trees to maintain shape and absorb moisture
4. Avoid direct heat sources when drying
