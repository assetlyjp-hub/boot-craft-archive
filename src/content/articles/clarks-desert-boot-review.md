## Overview

The Clarks Desert Boot is a chukka from clarks, made in England/Vietnam. Priced at $130, it features suede leather with cemented construction.

The Clarks Desert Boot is one of the most influential shoes ever designed. Created by Nathan Clark in 1950 after seeing crepe-soled boots worn by British officers in Cairo, its minimalist two-eyelet chukka design and soft crepe sole have made it a timeless wardrobe staple across generations and subcultures.

## Construction & Materials

- **Leather**: Suede
- **Construction**: Cemented
- **Sole**: Crepe
- **Last Shape**: Desert
- **Made In**: England/Vietnam

## Key Features

- Premium suede upper
- Natural crepe rubber sole
- Two-eyelet lacing
- Lightweight and flexible
- Iconic since 1950

## Fit & Sizing

As with most cemented boots, we recommend trying the Clarks Desert Boot in your Brannock size and potentially sizing down by half a size. The Desert last provides a balanced fit that accommodates most foot shapes.

## Value Assessment

At $130, the Clarks Desert Boot sits in the budget price range. For a cemented boot made in England/Vietnam with suede leather, this represents solid value in today's market.

## Verdict

The Clarks Desert Boot is a worthy addition to any footwear collection. clarks's commitment to quality materials and proven construction methods ensures this is a boot that will serve you well for years to come.
